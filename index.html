FAQ

Will quantum computing break blockchain?

Eventually, yes‚Äîbut the timeline matters. Quantum computers capable of breaking current cryptographic signatures will likely arrive in the coming years. At Quredge, quantum researchers and web3 builders collaborate on understanding the threat landscape and building solutions.

What is post-quantum cryptography?

PQC uses mathematical problems believed to resist quantum attacks‚Äîlattice problems, multivariate polynomials. Standards have been finalized, and the conversation now shifts to implementation. The residency brings together those who understand the theory with those building the infrastructure.

What is distributed quantum computing?

Rather than single quantum processors, distributed networks of quantum resources working together. Web3's decentralized coordination models offer unexpected frameworks for quantum resource sharing, privacy-preserving computation, and new governance structures.

Do I need deep technical background?

It depends on your track. Quantum researchers typically have PhDs or equivalent experience. Web3 builders bring blockchain expertise. Philosophers and artists bring conceptual frameworks. The magic happens when different types of expertise converge‚Äîcuriosity matters more than credentials.

Why transdisciplinary collaboration?

Quantum mechanics resists intuition. Metaphors unlock understanding. Historically, breakthroughs emerge at disciplinary boundaries‚ÄîFeynman diagrams, nature-inspired algorithms. Artists and philosophers help translate between quantum physicists and web3 builders.

What outcomes can I expect?

Depends on your role. Researchers: collaborative papers, code repositories. Builders: prototype quantum-safe protocols, partnerships. Philosophers: new frameworks, publications. All participants: lasting collaborations that continue years after the residency ends.
